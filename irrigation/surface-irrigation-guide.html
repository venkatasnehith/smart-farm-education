Surface Irrigation Methods

Understanding surface irrigation techniques and their applications

Last Updated: Mar 14, 2024 | 15 min read

Introduction to Surface Irrigation

Surface irrigation is one of the oldest and most widely used irrigation methods, where water is distributed across the field by gravity flow.

Key Benefits

  • Low energy requirements
  • Simple technology
  • Lower initial costs
  • Suitable for many soil types
  • Minimal maintenance needs

Types of Surface Irrigation

Basin Irrigation

  • Level plots with raised borders
  • Complete flooding of area
  • Ideal for rice cultivation
  • Good for close-growing crops

Furrow Irrigation

  • Parallel channels along crop rows
  • Water flows down slope
  • Suitable for row crops
  • Better water control

Border Strip Irrigation

  • Long rectangular strips
  • Gentle, uniform slope
  • Good for forage crops
  • Efficient water distribution

Land Preparation

Essential Steps

  • Proper land leveling
  • Field boundary establishment
  • Bund or border construction
  • Inlet structure installation
  • Drainage system setup

Common Mistakes

  • Inadequate leveling
  • Poor maintenance of bunds
  • Incorrect slope calculation
  • Insufficient drainage

Water Management

Application Rates

  • Flow rate calculation
  • Irrigation timing
  • Water depth control
  • Cut-off time determination

Efficiency Measures

  • Proper scheduling
  • Soil moisture monitoring
  • Runoff control
  • Maintenance of structures

Maintenance Requirements

Regular Tasks

  • Field releveling
  • Bund repair
  • Channel cleaning
  • Structure inspection
  • Erosion control

Troubleshooting

Common Issues

  • Uneven water distribution
  • Excessive runoff
  • Poor infiltration
  • Waterlogging

Solutions

  • Adjust flow rates
  • Improve land leveling
  • Modify field length
  • Enhance drainage
