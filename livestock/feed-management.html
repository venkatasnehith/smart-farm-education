Feed Management

Optimizing nutrition for healthy and productive livestock

Last Updated: Mar 14, 2024 | 18 min read

Understanding Feed Requirements

Proper feed management is crucial for livestock health, productivity, and profitability. Different species and life stages require specific nutritional considerations.

Essential Nutrients

  • Proteins
  • Carbohydrates
  • Fats
  • Vitamins
  • Minerals
  • Water

Factors Affecting Requirements

  • Age
  • Weight
  • Production stage
  • Environmental conditions
  • Health status

Species-Specific Feed Guidelines

Cattle

Stage Protein (%) Energy (Mcal/kg) Feed Intake (% BW)
Growing 12-14 2.4-2.6 2.5-3.0
Lactating 16-18 2.8-3.0 3.5-4.0
Dry 10-12 2.2-2.4 2.0-2.5

Stage Protein (%) Energy (Mcal/kg) Feed Intake (% BW)

Feed Storage and Management

Storage Guidelines

  • Keep feed in dry, well-ventilated areas
  • Protect from pests and rodents
  • Rotate stock (First In, First Out)
  • Monitor for mold and spoilage
  • Maintain proper temperature

Common Storage Mistakes

  • Improper moisture control
  • Poor ventilation
  • Inadequate pest control
  • Mixed feed storage

Feed Quality Assessment

Quality Indicators

  • Physical appearance
  • Smell
  • Moisture content
  • Nutrient analysis
  • Contamination levels

Testing Requirements

  • Regular nutrient analysis
  • Mycotoxin screening
  • Moisture testing
  • Protein content verification

Feed Cost Management

Cost Reduction Strategies

  • Bulk purchasing
  • Seasonal buying
  • Alternative feed sources
  • Waste reduction
  • Feed efficiency monitoring

Record Keeping

Essential Records

  • Feed purchases and costs
  • Feed analysis results
  • Storage conditions
  • Consumption rates
  • Animal performance data
